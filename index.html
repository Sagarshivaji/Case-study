Helping Users Understand Missed Turns

A UX Enhancement for Navigation Apps

Role UX Designer & Researcher
Duration 1 months
Tools Figma, Miro, Hotjar

Overview

The Challenge

Most navigation apps, including Google Maps, automatically reroute users the moment they take a wrong turn. While this is helpful for quickly getting back on track, it lacks one important detail: the app does not show what the user missed.

Project Goals

There is an opportunity to enhance the user experience by clearly showing the missed portion of the route — allowing users to recognize their deviation, understand the change, and regain a sense of control

Competitive Analysis

None of the major navigation apps (Waze, Apple Maps, TomTom, HERE WeGo, or Sygic) provide a visual indicator for missed turns that remains on screen after the user makes a mistake. They all prioritize quickly updating the new route, but fail to show what was missed, which can be confusing for users trying to mentally map their surroundings./p>

Key Findings

Pain Point #1

1. No Visual Indication of What Was Missed “I don’t know what I missed ,it just reroutes.”

Apps instantly remove the original route without showing the missed turn. Users can't mentally map or learn from the mistake.

Pain Point #2

2. Confusion During Rerouting “It reroutes so fast, I lose track of where I am.” Sudden change in route can feel disorienting. Especially stressful while driving or in unfamiliar areas.

Pain Point #3

Increased Cognitive Load While Driving “It makes me anxious when the map shifts and I don’t know why.” Drivers have to reorient themselves quickly. Adds mental stress, especially in complex intersections or highways.

Opportunity

Current systems don’t let users choose the type or length of feedback. No toggle or user control over rerouting behavior.

User Testing

Usability Testing

Usability Testing Conducted moderated sessions with 8 participants to evaluate the effectiveness of the new missed-turn recovery flow. 92% Success Rate in completing navigation correction tasks ⏱ 28s Average Time Reduction in route recovery compared to the old flow Users reported improved clarity, control, and reduced confusion when a turn was missed

92% Success Rate
28s Avg. Time Reduction

A/B Testing

Ran a 2-week live test with 10,000 users, comparing the old automatic rerouting experience with the new interactive recovery design. +32% Increase in successful reroutes using the “Go-Back / Continue” choice -41% Reduction in drop-off rate after a missed turn Higher engagement and satisfaction, especially on unfamiliar routes

+32% Conversion
-41% Drop-off Rate

Results & Impact

61% of users felt more confident navigating after missing a turn
40% reduction in user frustration during missed turn scenarios (measured via post-test surveys)
+0.2⭐ improvement in navigation experience ratings (average rose from 4.1 to 4.4)

Key Learnings

1.Giving users control over rerouting increases trust and reduces reliance on voice instructions

2.Highlighting missed routes visually (in red) helps users understand and learn from errors

3.Offering a clear binary choice (Go Back / Continue) reduces decision fatigue and supports faster re-engagement
